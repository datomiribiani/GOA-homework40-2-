The first mechanical computer was called the Analytical Engine, and it was designed in 1837 by an English mathematician and inventor named Charles Babbage. This machine was never fully built during his lifetime, but it had all the basic elements of a modern computer, such as: An arithmetic logic unit (to perform calculations), Memory (to store numbers), And an input/output system (using punched cards). Because of this, Charles Babbage is often called the "Father of the Computer." His ideas laid the foundation for how computers would work more than 100 years later.
